Iterative Local-LLM Research

Exact tooling for eight open problems.

Each problem here gets what the research methodology actually calls for: an exact verifier, a bounded search or enumeration, and reproducible certificates — falsifiable artifacts that survive contact with a checker. These are computational research scaffolds and verified bounded results, built one iteration at a time. They are not proofs of the conjectures, which remain open.

Honesty note. Every problem below is a famous open question. Nothing here claims to solve one. The deliverable is tooling: an independent checker, an exact search to a stated bound, and the structural data (congruence classes, infeasibility certificates, small-instance classifications) that genuine progress is built from.

The method

Every problem follows the same loop: exact generation → independent verification → congruence/obstruction mining. The verifier is always a separate codebase from the generator, uses exact arithmetic only, and is the sole arbiter of truth. Partial, bounded, or negative results count — and are reported as exactly that.
